## 1. Overview: The Travnook Sales Process

### What Happens Before You Get the Lead
Our qualification bot handles the initial contact. It collects:
- Destination country
- Nationality
- Travel dates
- Emirates ID validity
- Bank account / financial readiness

Once qualified, the lead is transferred to YOU — the sales specialist.

### Your Job: Convert Qualified Leads into Paying Customers
Your pipeline has 6 stages:
1. **Warm Handoff** — Greet the client, confirm their needs
2. **Pricing & Requirements** — Share the price and document list
3. **Document Collection** — Collect all required documents
4. **Payment Processing** — Handle payment via transfer or link
5. **Application & Submission** — Submit and track the application
6. **Delivery & Close** — Deliver the visa, congratulate the client

## 3. Stage-by-Stage Sales Guide

### 3.1 Warm Handoff Greeting

**Goal**: Make the client feel welcomed and confirm their needs.

**Best Practices** (from top-performing agents):
- Introduce yourself by name
- Reference Travnook Travel specifically
- Immediately confirm the service they need
- Don't make them repeat info they already told the bot

**English Example:**
> "Hello! This is Ali from Travnook Travel. How can I help you?"

**Arabic Example:**
> "اهلا و سهلا، انا علي من ترافنوك للسفر. كيف اقدر اساعدك؟"

**❌ What NOT to do:**
- Don't use generic greetings without introducing yourself
- Don't ask questions the bot already asked
- Don't make the client wait more than a few minutes after transfer

### 3.2 Pricing & Requirements Communication

**Goal**: Present the price confidently and list all required documents clearly.

**Best Practices:**
- State the price clearly in one message
- List ALL required documents in a clean bullet format
- Mention processing time upfront
- If client asks 'how much', answer immediately — don't deflect

**Effective Price Communication (from real conversations):**
> "The Saudi tourist visa is 750 AED. We need:
> - Passport copy (clear scan)
> - Emirates ID copy (front & back)
> - Personal photo (white background)
> Processing takes 1-3 working days."

**For Arabic clients:**
> "فيزا السعودية السياحية 750 درهم. الأوراق المطلوبة:
> - صورة جواز السفر
> - صورة الهوية الإماراتية
> - صورة شخصية
> المدة من يوم إلى 3 أيام عمل"

### 3.3 Document Collection

**Goal**: Collect all required documents efficiently.

**Best Practices:**
- Verify each document as it arrives
- If a document is unclear, explain EXACTLY what's wrong
- Be patient — clients sometimes take days to gather everything
- Send gentle reminders after 24 hours of silence

**Common Document Issues:**
| Issue | How to Handle |
|-------|---------------|
| Blurry passport scan | "The passport photo is a bit blurry. Could you please take a clearer scan?" |
| Wrong photo format | "We need a photo with white background. Could you send one?" |
| Missing EID back | "I received the front of your Emirates ID. Could you send the back as well?" |
| Expired EID | "Your Emirates ID appears to be expired. We need a valid one to proceed." |

### 3.4 Payment Processing

**Goal**: Collect payment smoothly.

**Payment Options to Offer:**
1. **Bank Transfer** — Direct to Travnook account (no extra fees)
2. **Payment Link** — Card payment (small processing fee ~25 AED)

**Best Practices:**
- Always offer both options
- For payment links, mention the card fee upfront
- Confirm payment receipt within 1 hour
- If payment is delayed, follow up gently

**Example (from real conversations):**
> "For payment, you can do bank transfer or I can send a payment link (25 AED card fee). Which do you prefer?"

> Arabic: "تحويل بنكي او ابعتلك رابط دفع عن طريق بطاقة بس بيوصلك 25 درهم زيادة رسوم دفع الكتروني"

### 3.5 Application Submission & Follow-up

**Goal**: Submit the application and keep the client informed.

**Best Practices:**
- Confirm submission immediately
- Provide realistic timeline
- Update every 2-3 days or when status changes
- If there are delays, inform proactively — don't wait for client to ask

### 3.6 Visa Delivery & Closing

**Goal**: Deliver the visa and leave a great impression.

**Best Practices:**
- Share the visa document immediately when ready
- Congratulate the client warmly
- Mention you're available for future needs
- Ask for referrals (subtly)

**Example:**
> "Great news! Your visa has been approved ✈️ I'm sending it now. Safe travels and feel free to reach out anytime you need help!"

> Arabic: "مبروك! الفيزا جاهزة ✈️ ارسلها لك الحين. رحلة سعيدة وإذا تحتاج أي شي بالمستقبل لا تتردد!"

## 4. Objection Handling Playbook

### 4.1 Price Objection
**Client says**: "That's too expensive" / "I can get it cheaper"

**How to handle:**
1. Acknowledge their concern (don't dismiss)
2. Highlight the value: speed, reliability, track record
3. Mention discounts if applicable (referral, group, repeat customer)
4. Never badmouth competitors

**Example Response:**
> "I understand the concern. Our pricing includes complete processing, document review, embassy follow-up, and we have a high approval rate. We've served over 2.5 million customers since 2015."

### 4.2 Need Time to Decide
**Client says**: "Let me think about it" / "I'll get back to you"

**How to handle:**
1. Be understanding — never pressure
2. Offer to keep their file ready
3. Follow up in 24-48 hours

**Example Response:**
> "No rush at all! I'll keep your details saved. Just message when ready and we'll process immediately."

### 4.3 Comparing with Other Agencies
**Client says**: "I'm checking with other agencies too"

**How to handle:**
1. Respect their decision to compare
2. Highlight Travnook's differentiators
3. Offer to match or discuss pricing if they share competitor quotes

### 4.4 Document Difficulty
**Client says**: "I don't have this document" / "It's hard to get"

**How to handle:**
1. Explain why the document is needed
2. Guide them on how to obtain it
3. Offer alternatives if possible
4. Be patient — this is a common blocker

## 5. Follow-up Strategy

### Timing Rules
| Situation | When to Follow Up | Message Style |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| After sending pricing | 24 hours | Gentle check-in |
| Partial documents received | 24 hours | Specific reminder about missing docs |
| Payment pending | 24-48 hours | Friendly nudge |
| After submission | Every 2-3 days | Status update |
| Client went silent | 48 hours, then 72 hours | Final gentle reminder, then stop |

### Follow-up Message Examples
> **24hr check-in**: "Hi! Just checking in — did you get a chance to review the requirements?"
> **Doc reminder**: "Hi! We're just waiting for your passport copy to proceed. Could you send it when you get a chance?"
> **Payment nudge**: "Hi! Once the payment is confirmed, we'll submit your application right away 👍"

## 6. Language-Specific Communication Tips

### English Communication
- Professional but warm tone
- Use clear, simple language (many clients are non-native English speakers)
- Avoid jargon
- Use bullet points for document lists

### Arabic Communication (GCC Style)
- Use GCC Arabic, NOT Egyptian dialect
- Common greetings: اهلا و سهلا, مرحبا, تكرم عينك
- Use polite forms: لو سمحت, ممكن
- Be warm with expressions like يلا, تمام, ان شاء الله
- Avoid overly formal/stiff language
- Match the client's dialect when possible
